# How to Clean Your Washing Machine Naturally

Most people wash their clothes every week, but very few think about cleaning the washing machine itself.

Although it constantly uses water and detergent, your washing machine isn't self-cleaning. Every laundry cycle leaves behind detergent residue, lint, body oils, fabric softener, dirt, and minerals from hard water. Over time, these deposits collect inside the drum, rubber seal, detergent drawer, filters, and drainage system.

Eventually, this buildup can cause unpleasant odors, mold growth, poor washing performance, and even leave freshly washed clothes smelling musty.

The good news is that keeping your washing machine clean naturally doesn't require much effort. With a simple monthly cleaning routine and a few good habits, you can keep your machine running efficiently while extending its lifespan.

---

# Why You Should Clean Your Washing Machine

Cleaning your washing machine regularly offers several benefits beyond just removing bad smells.

A clean machine helps:

- Remove detergent and fabric softener buildup
- Prevent mold and mildew growth
- Eliminate unpleasant odors
- Improve washing performance
- Protect internal components
- Keep clothes smelling fresh
- Extend the life of your appliance
- Reduce maintenance costs

Think of it as preventive care for one of the hardest-working appliances in your home.

---

# Signs Your Washing Machine Needs Cleaning

Your washing machine may be overdue for cleaning if you notice any of these signs:

- Musty smell when opening the door
- Clothes don't smell fresh after washing
- Black spots around the rubber gasket
- Soap residue inside the drum
- Mold in the detergent compartment
- White mineral deposits
- Longer wash cycles
- Water draining slowly
- Visible lint or dirt buildup

Ignoring these warning signs can reduce your machine's efficiency over time.

# Natural Cleaning Essentials

Before starting, gather these simple items:

- Home Rituals Washing Machine Cleaner
- Soft microfiber cloth
- Small soft brush
- Warm water
- Soft sponge
- Rubber gloves (optional)

For light exterior cleaning, you can also keep a clean towel nearby for drying surfaces.

---

# Step 1 — Empty the Machine

Remove all clothes from the drum.

Check carefully for forgotten tissues, coins, hairpins, or lint that may have collected inside.

Starting with an empty machine ensures every part receives proper cleaning.

---

# Step 2 — Clean the Detergent Drawer

The detergent drawer often hides residue and mold.

Remove the drawer if your machine allows.

Wash it with warm water and gently scrub away detergent deposits using a soft brush.

Pay close attention to corners and hidden compartments where buildup commonly occurs.

Dry completely before reinstalling.

---

# Step 3 — Wipe the Rubber Door Seal

Front-load washing machines often trap moisture inside the rubber gasket.

Carefully pull back the folds and remove:

- Dirt
- Hair
- Soap residue
- Mold
- Lint

Use a damp microfiber cloth to wipe every fold.

After cleaning, dry the seal thoroughly to prevent future mold growth.

---

# Step 4 — Run a Natural Cleaning Cycle

Follow the directions on your Home Rituals Washing Machine Cleaner.

The formula is designed to help loosen detergent residue, mineral deposits, odor-causing bacteria, and hidden grime without harsh scrubbing.

Choose the hottest empty wash cycle available on your machine.

Allow the cycle to complete fully.

---

# Step 5 — Clean the Drum

Once the cleaning cycle finishes, wipe the inside of the drum using a clean microfiber cloth.

Remove any remaining moisture around the door opening.

Leaving the machine dry helps prevent bacteria and mildew from returning.

---

# Step 6 — Leave the Door Open

One of the simplest maintenance habits is also one of the most effective.

Leave the washing machine door slightly open after every wash.

This allows moisture to escape and keeps mold and mildew from developing.

---

# Front Load vs Top Load Maintenance

## Front Load Washing Machines

Front-load washers require extra attention because moisture often remains trapped.

Focus on cleaning:

- Rubber gasket
- Detergent drawer
- Drain filter
- Drum
- Door glass

Monthly maintenance is highly recommended.

---

## Top Load Washing Machines

Top-load machines generally dry more quickly but still collect detergent residue.

Clean:

- Drum
- Agitator (if present)
- Lid
- Fabric softener dispenser
- Detergent compartment

Running a maintenance cycle once every month keeps them performing efficiently.

---

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these habits that shorten the life of your washing machine:

- Using excessive detergent
- Leaving wet clothes inside for hours
- Closing the door immediately after washing
- Ignoring the drain filter
- Never cleaning the detergent drawer
- Skipping monthly maintenance

Small daily habits make a big difference over time.

---

# Monthly Washing Machine Checklist

✔ Empty the machine

✔ Clean the detergent drawer

✔ Wipe the rubber seal

✔ Run a cleaning cycle

✔ Inspect the filter

✔ Wipe the drum dry

✔ Leave the door slightly open

Following this checklist every month keeps your machine cleaner for longer.

---

# Pro Tips for a Fresher Washing Machine

- Measure detergent carefully.
- Don't overload the drum.
- Remove laundry immediately after washing.
- Dry the rubber gasket after every cycle.
- Keep the detergent drawer clean.
- Leave the machine door slightly open.
- Clean the machine every month.

These simple habits reduce odor and improve washing performance.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## How often should I clean my washing machine?

Once every month is ideal for most households. If you wash clothes daily or have a large family, cleaning every two to three weeks may be beneficial.

---

## Why does my washing machine smell bad?

Odors are usually caused by detergent residue, trapped moisture, mold, mildew, and bacteria that build up inside the machine.

---

## Is natural cleaning safe for my washing machine?

Yes. Following your appliance manufacturer's recommendations and using products specifically designed for washing machine maintenance helps keep your machine in excellent condition.

---

## Can a dirty washing machine affect my clothes?

Absolutely.

Residue and bacteria inside the drum can transfer unpleasant odors to freshly washed clothes, making them smell less clean.

---

## Does regular cleaning improve washing performance?

Yes.

A clean washing machine allows better water circulation, improves detergent effectiveness, and helps clothes come out cleaner and fresher.
